Sinigang Hipon

Ingredients

  • 500g shrimp, peeled and deveined (with shells on for flavor)
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 pack of fresh or frozen tamarind (or 1-2 tbsp sinigang mix)
  • 1-2 long green chili peppers (optional)
  • 1-2 radishes (labanos), sliced
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup sitaw (string beans), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach)
  • 1 liter water
  • Fish sauce or salt, to taste
  • 1-2 tbsp sugar (optional, to balance the sourness)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the tamarind base: If using fresh tamarind, boil it with water until the tamarind softens. Once softened, mash the tamarind and strain the juice to get a sour base. If using sinigang mix, simply dissolve it in the water.
  2. Start the soup: In a large pot, bring 1 liter of water to a boil. Add the onions and tomatoes, and cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Add the tamarind base: Add the strained tamarind juice or the sinigang mix to the pot. Stir well and bring it to a simmer.
  4. Add vegetables: Add the radishes, eggplant, and string beans (sitaw). Cook for about 10 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Add shrimp: Add the shrimp and green chili peppers (if using) to the pot. Cook for another 5-7 minutes, or until the shrimp turn pink and are fully cooked.
  6. Season: Taste the broth and season with fish sauce, salt, and sugar (optional) to adjust the sourness and flavor to your liking.
  7. Add kangkong: Finally, add the kangkong (water spinach) and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until wilted.
  8. Serve: Serve hot with steamed rice on the side.
