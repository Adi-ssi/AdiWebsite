Steak

Ingredients

  • 2 pieces of steak (ribeye, sirloin, or any cut of your choice)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (or butter)
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1-2 sprigs of fresh rosemary or thyme (optional)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp butter (for finishing)
  • Optional: 1 tbsp soy sauce or Worcestershire sauce for added flavor

Instructions

  1. Prepare the steak: Remove the steak from the refrigerator 20-30 minutes before cooking to allow it to come to room temperature. This ensures even cooking. Pat the steak dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture, which helps achieve a nice sear.
  2. Season the steak: Generously season both sides of the steak with salt and freshly ground black pepper. If you prefer, you can also marinate the steak for 30 minutes with a bit of soy sauce or Worcestershire sauce for extra flavor.
  3. Heat the pan: In a heavy skillet (preferably cast-iron), heat the olive oil over medium-high heat until the oil starts to shimmer. You can also use butter if you prefer a richer taste.
  4. Cook the steak: Add the steak to the hot pan. Let it cook undisturbed for 3-4 minutes (for medium-rare), or until a nice crust forms. Flip the steak and add the smashed garlic and herbs (rosemary or thyme) to the pan. Continue cooking the steak for another 3-4 minutes, depending on your preferred doneness (adjust cooking time based on thickness and doneness preference).
  5. Add butter (optional): During the last minute of cooking, add a tablespoon of butter to the pan and spoon the melted butter over the steak for added richness.
  6. Rest the steak: Once the steak is cooked to your liking, remove it from the pan and let it rest on a plate for about 5-10 minutes. Resting helps the juices redistribute, making the steak more tender and juicy.
  7. Serve: Slice the steak against the grain and serve immediately. Enjoy with your favorite sides like mashed potatoes, vegetables, or a simple salad.
