Tocilog

Ingredients

For the Tocino (Sweet Cured Pork):

  • 500g pork belly or pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp garlic, minced
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil (for frying)

For the Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice):

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

For the Itlog (Fried Egg):

  • 4 eggs
  • Oil for frying

For garnish:

  • Sliced tomatoes (optional)
  • Cucumber slices (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Tocino (Sweet Cured Pork):
    1. Marinate the pork: In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, vinegar, brown sugar, minced garlic, salt, and ground black pepper. Add the sliced pork to the marinade and mix well to coat. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes, but for best results, marinate it for a few hours or overnight in the refrigerator.
    2. Cook the tocino: Heat 1 tablespoon of cooking oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the marinated pork and cook for 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally until the pork is cooked through and lightly caramelized. If the pork is browning too quickly, you can add a little water to cook it through. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice):
    1. Sauté garlic: In the same pan, add 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil. Add the minced garlic and sauté over medium heat until golden brown and fragrant.
    2. Fry the rice: Add the day-old rice to the pan and stir-fry for 3-5 minutes. Break up any rice clumps and continue frying until the rice is heated through and slightly crispy. Season with salt to taste, then remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. Prepare the Itlog (Fried Egg):
    1. Fry the eggs: In a separate pan, heat oil over medium heat. Crack the eggs into the pan and fry them to your preferred doneness. Typically, sunny-side-up eggs are served with Tocilog. Season with a little salt and pepper. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  4. Assemble the Tocilog:
    1. Serve: On a plate, serve a portion of sinangag (garlic fried rice), top with the cooked tocino, and add a fried egg on the side. Garnish with sliced tomatoes or cucumber if desired.

Enjoy your Tocilog, a delicious and flavorful Filipino breakfast that’s sure to keep you energized throughout the day!
