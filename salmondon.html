Japanese Salmon Don

Ingredients

  • 2 fresh salmon fillets (skinless, boneless)
  • 2 cups cooked sushi rice or short-grain rice
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds (optional)
  • 1-2 teaspoons nori (seaweed) strips (optional)
  • Pickled ginger (optional)
  • Fresh lemon wedges (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Salmon:
    • Slice the salmon fillets into thin, bite-sized pieces (about 1/2 inch wide). You can serve it raw, or if you prefer, lightly sear the salmon in a hot pan with a little oil for about 1-2 minutes per side, keeping the inside raw or medium-rare.
  2. Make the Sushi Rice:
    • If you haven’t cooked rice yet, prepare sushi rice by rinsing the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Cook according to your rice cooker or stovetop method. After cooking, mix 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar, 1 tablespoon of sugar, and a pinch of salt into the rice while it’s still warm. Let it cool slightly.
  3. Assemble the Bowl:
    • In a bowl, place a serving of sushi rice as the base. Drizzle with a little sesame oil and soy sauce for flavor.
  4. Top with Salmon:
    • Arrange the raw or seared salmon slices on top of the rice.
  5. Add Toppings:
    • Add thin cucumber slices and avocado on the side. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and nori strips for extra texture and flavor.
  6. Optional Garnishes:
    • Serve with pickled ginger on the side and a lemon wedge for squeezing over the top.
  7. Serve:
    • Enjoy immediately for a fresh, delicious meal!
