Chicken Silog

Ingredients

For the Chicken:

  • 2 pieces of chicken breasts or thighs (boneless or bone-in, depending on preference)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp calamansi or lemon juice
  • 1 tsp garlic powder or minced garlic
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil (for frying)

For the Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice):

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

For the Itlog (Fried Egg):

  • 4 eggs
  • Oil for frying

For garnish:

  • Sliced tomatoes (optional)
  • Cucumber slices (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chicken:
    1. Marinate the chicken: In a bowl, combine soy sauce, calamansi (or lemon juice), garlic powder (or minced garlic), and ground black pepper. Add the chicken and marinate for at least 15 minutes, or up to 1 hour for more flavor.
    2. Cook the chicken: Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Fry the marinated chicken for 5-7 minutes on each side (depending on thickness) until it is golden brown and cooked through. If using bone-in chicken, ensure the meat is fully cooked before removing from the pan. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice):
    1. Sauté garlic: In the same pan (or another), heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté until it becomes golden brown and fragrant.
    2. Fry the rice: Add the day-old rice to the pan, breaking up any clumps. Stir-fry for 3-5 minutes until the rice is heated through and slightly crispy. Season with salt to taste, then remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. Prepare the Itlog (Fried Egg):
    1. Fry the eggs: In a separate pan, heat oil over medium heat. Crack the eggs into the pan and fry them sunny-side up or to your preferred doneness. Season with salt and pepper. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  4. Assemble the Chicken Silog:
    1. Serve: On a plate, serve a portion of sinangag (garlic fried rice), top with the crispy fried chicken, and add a fried egg on the side. Garnish with sliced tomatoes or cucumber if desired.

Enjoy your Chicken Silog, a hearty and satisfying Filipino breakfast to kickstart your day!
