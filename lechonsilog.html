Lechon Silog

Ingredients

For the Lechon (Roast Pork):

  • 1 cup leftover lechon (or lechon kawali), chopped into small pieces
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp salt (optional)

For the Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice):

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

For the Itlog (Fried Egg):

  • 4 eggs
  • Oil for frying

For garnish:

  • Sliced tomatoes (optional)
  • Cucumber slices (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Lechon:
    1. Reheat the lechon: If you have leftover lechon (or lechon kawali), chop it into small pieces. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the chopped lechon and fry it for 3-5 minutes or until it’s crispy and slightly golden. Season with a little salt and ground black pepper to taste. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice):
    1. Sauté garlic: In the same pan, add 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil. Add the minced garlic and sauté over medium heat until golden brown and fragrant.
    2. Fry the rice: Add the day-old rice to the pan and stir-fry for 3-5 minutes, breaking up any clumps. Continue frying until the rice is heated through and slightly crispy. Season with salt to taste, then remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. Prepare the Itlog (Fried Egg):
    1. Fry the eggs: In a separate pan, heat oil over medium heat. Crack the eggs into the pan and fry them to your desired doneness. Typically, sunny-side-up eggs are served with Lechon Silog. Season with salt and pepper. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  4. Assemble the Lechon Silog:
    1. Serve: On a plate, serve a portion of sinangag (garlic fried rice), top it with the crispy lechon, and add a fried egg on the side. Garnish with sliced tomatoes or cucumber if desired.

Enjoy your Lechon Silog, a delicious and hearty Filipino breakfast dish that's perfect for a satisfying start to your day!
