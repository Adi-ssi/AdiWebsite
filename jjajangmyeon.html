Jjajangmyeon

Ingredients

  • 200g (7 oz) fresh or dried wheat noodles (or ramen noodles)
  • 1/2 lb (225g) pork belly or pork shoulder, diced into small cubes (you can also use beef or chicken)
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 1/2 zucchini, diced
  • 1/2 potato, peeled and diced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 tablespoons black bean paste (chunjang)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons garlic, minced
  • 2 cups water or chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (mixed with 2 tablespoons water to make a slurry)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cucumber (for garnish, optional)
  • Sesame oil (for garnish, optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook the Noodles:
    • Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the noodles and cook according to the package instructions, usually for 3-5 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Pork and Vegetables:
    • Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat.
    • Add the diced pork and cook until itâ€™s browned and crispy, about 5-7 minutes.
    • Add the diced onion, zucchini, and potato to the pan, cooking for an additional 3-4 minutes until the vegetables soften.
  3. Make the Sauce:
    • Push the meat and vegetables to one side of the pan. In the empty side, add the black bean paste (chunjang) and let it fry for about 1-2 minutes to release its flavors.
    • Add sugar, soy sauce, and garlic. Stir everything together until the paste and vegetables are well combined.
    • Pour in the water or chicken broth, stirring to mix everything well. Bring it to a simmer and let it cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are fully cooked.
  4. Thicken the Sauce:
    • Once the vegetables are soft, add the cornstarch slurry (cornstarch mixed with water) to the pan. Stir well and cook for another 1-2 minutes, until the sauce thickens.
  5. Combine the Noodles and Sauce:
    • Place the cooked noodles in serving bowls. Pour the black bean sauce mixture over the noodles, ensuring they are well-coated.
  6. Serve:
    • Garnish with thinly sliced cucumber for freshness and drizzle with a little sesame oil if desired.
