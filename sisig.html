Sisig

Ingredients

  • 1/2 kg pork belly (or a mix of pork head, ears, and liver for a more traditional version)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2-3 tbsp calamansi or lemon juice
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 bird’s eye chili peppers (siling labuyo), chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 egg (optional)
  • 1 tbsp mayonnaise (optional, for creaminess)
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • Fresh calamansi or lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the pork: Boil the pork belly (or mix of pork head, ears, and liver) in a large pot of water for 1-1.5 hours, or until tender. Add a pinch of salt to the water to season the pork. Once tender, remove from the pot and let it cool. Once cooled, cut the pork into small cubes or bite-sized pieces.
  2. Grill the pork (optional): For a smoky flavor, grill the boiled pork pieces on a charcoal grill or use a grill pan until they are slightly charred and crispy on the outside. This step is optional but adds great flavor to your sisig.
  3. Sauté the sisig: In a large pan, heat the cooking oil and butter over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté until golden and fragrant. Then, add the chopped onions and cook until softened.
  4. Combine the pork and seasoning: Add the cubed pork into the pan with the onions and garlic. Cook for 5-7 minutes, allowing the pork to crisp up and absorb the flavors.
  5. Add the seasoning: Add the soy sauce, calamansi or lemon juice, ground black pepper, and bird’s eye chili peppers. Stir well to combine all the flavors. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and sugar if needed.
  6. Optional egg and mayonnaise: For a creamy twist, crack the egg into the pan and scramble it into the sisig mixture until cooked. You can also stir in 1 tablespoon of mayonnaise for added creaminess and texture.
  7. Serve: Serve the sisig on a sizzling hot plate if you have one, or just on a regular plate. Top with a wedge of calamansi or lemon and enjoy with steamed rice.
