Tonkatsu

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless pork loin chops or pork tenderloin (about 1-inch thick)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs (Japanese breadcrumbs)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Tonkatsu sauce (store-bought or homemade)

For Homemade Tonkatsu Sauce

  • 3 tablespoons ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon honey or sugar

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pork:
    • Season the pork chops with salt and pepper on both sides. You can also tenderize the pork by gently pounding it with a meat mallet to make it more tender.
  2. Breading Station:
    • Set up three shallow dishes:
      • In the first dish, place the flour.
      • In the second dish, beat the eggs.
      • In the third dish, place the panko breadcrumbs.
  3. Coat the Pork:
    • Dredge each pork chop in flour, making sure to shake off any excess. Then, dip it into the beaten eggs, ensuring it is fully coated. Finally, press the pork into the panko breadcrumbs, making sure it is evenly covered.
  4. Fry the Tonkatsu:
    • Heat about 1 inch of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot (around 350°F or 175°C), carefully place the breaded pork chops in the oil. Fry for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until golden brown and crispy. If needed, fry the pork in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan.
  5. Drain:
    • Remove the pork from the oil and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.
  6. Make the Sauce (if using homemade):
    • In a small bowl, combine the ketchup, Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, Dijon mustard, and honey or sugar. Stir well to combine.
  7. Serve:
    • Slice the tonkatsu into strips and serve with a drizzle of tonkatsu sauce on top. You can also serve it with shredded cabbage, steamed rice, and miso soup on the side for a complete meal.
