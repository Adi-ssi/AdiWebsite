Taho

Ingredients

  • 1 block of silken tofu (around 300g)
  • 1/2 cup sago pearls (tapioca pearls)
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar (or muscovado sugar)
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Sago (Tapioca Pearls):
    • Cook the sago pearls: In a pot, bring water to a boil and add the sago pearls. Cook for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the pearls become translucent and soft. Make sure the water doesn’t boil over. If necessary, add more water to keep the pearls submerged.
    • Drain the sago: Once the pearls are cooked, drain them and rinse with cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the Syrup:
    • Make the sago syrup: In a small saucepan, combine the brown sugar and water. Heat over medium heat and stir until the sugar dissolves completely. Once it starts to simmer, let it cook for another 2-3 minutes. Optionally, you can add vanilla extract for a hint of flavor. Set the syrup aside.
  3. Prepare the Tofu:
    • Heat the tofu: Gently steam or warm the silken tofu by placing it in a bowl over a pot of hot water for 5-10 minutes, until it becomes soft and warm. If you don’t have a steamer, you can microwave the tofu for about 30 seconds to 1 minute.
  4. Assemble the Taho:
    • Layer the Taho: In serving cups, add a few tablespoons of the cooked sago pearls. Then add a generous scoop of warm silken tofu on top of the pearls.
    • Pour the syrup: Drizzle the warm sago syrup over the tofu and pearls to your desired sweetness.
  5. Serve and enjoy: Mix everything together and enjoy your delicious homemade Taho!

Enjoy this sweet and comforting Filipino treat, perfect for any time of the day!
