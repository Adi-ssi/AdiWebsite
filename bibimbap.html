Bibimbap

Ingredients

  • For the Rice: 2 cups cooked short-grain rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • For the Vegetables and Protein:
    • 1/2 lb (225g) ground beef (or chicken, tofu, or beef strips)
    • 1 small zucchini, julienned
    • 1/2 carrot, julienned
    • 1 cup spinach, blanched and squeezed dry
    • 1/2 cup shiitake mushrooms (or any mushrooms), sliced
    • 2-3 eggs (1 egg per serving)
  • For the Sauce:
    • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste)
    • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional)
    • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar (optional)
    • 1 teaspoon garlic, minced
  • For Garnish:
    • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
    • 1 teaspoon sesame oil (optional)
    • Fresh spinach or other greens (optional)
    • Kimchi (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Rice:
    • Cook the rice according to package instructions, or use leftover rice. Set aside.
  2. Cook the Ground Beef:
    • In a pan, heat 1 tablespoon of sesame oil over medium heat. Add the ground beef, season with a pinch of salt and pepper, and cook until browned, breaking it up into small crumbles. Set aside.
  3. Prepare the Vegetables:
    • For the zucchini and carrots: Lightly sauté them in a pan with a little oil until soft but still a bit crisp (about 2-3 minutes).
    • For the spinach: Blanch the spinach in boiling water for about 30 seconds, then drain and squeeze out excess water. Season with a pinch of salt and a drizzle of sesame oil.
    • For the mushrooms: Sauté the sliced mushrooms in a pan with a bit of oil for 2-3 minutes, until tender.
  4. Fry the Eggs:
    • In a separate pan, fry the eggs sunny side up or to your preference. The runny yolk adds to the texture of the bibimbap.
  5. Make the Sauce:
    • In a small bowl, mix together the gochujang, sesame oil, soy sauce, sugar, rice vinegar, and minced garlic until smooth.
  6. Assemble the Bibimbap:
    • Place a serving of warm rice at the bottom of a bowl.
    • Arrange each vegetable and the cooked beef (or other protein) on top of the rice in a circle around the bowl.
    • Place a fried egg in the center.
  7. Serve:
    • Drizzle the gochujang sauce over the top and garnish with sesame seeds and additional sesame oil. Serve with kimchi on the side if desired.
  8. To Eat:
    • Mix everything together in the bowl until the rice is well-coated with the sauce, and enjoy the comforting, spicy, and savory flavors of Bibimbap!
