Tapsilog

Ingredients

For the Tapa (marinated beef):

  • 500g beef sirloin or flank steak, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil (for frying)

For the Sinangag (garlic fried rice):

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

For the Itlog (fried egg):

  • 4 eggs
  • Oil for frying

For garnish:

  • Sliced tomatoes (optional)
  • Cucumber slices (optional)
  • Pickled papaya or atchara (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Tapa (beef):
    1. Marinate the beef: In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, vinegar, brown sugar, minced garlic, ground black pepper, and salt. Add the sliced beef to the marinade and mix well to coat. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes, but for best results, marinate for a few hours or overnight in the refrigerator.
    2. Cook the tapa: In a pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Once hot, add the marinated beef and cook for about 5-7 minutes or until the beef is tender and slightly caramelized, stirring occasionally to prevent burning. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Sinangag (garlic fried rice):
    1. Sauté garlic: In the same pan, add 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté until golden and fragrant.
    2. Fry the rice: Add the day-old rice and stir-fry for 3-5 minutes, breaking up any clumps. Season with salt to taste and continue to fry until the rice is heated through and slightly crispy. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. Prepare the Itlog (fried egg):
    1. Fry the eggs: In a separate pan, heat oil over medium heat. Crack the eggs into the pan, one at a time, and fry to your desired doneness (sunny-side up is traditional). Season with a little salt and pepper. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  4. Assemble the Tapsilog:
    1. Serve: On a plate, serve a portion of sinangag (garlic fried rice), top with the cooked tapa (beef), and add a fried egg on the side. Garnish with sliced tomatoes, cucumber, or pickled papaya if desired.
