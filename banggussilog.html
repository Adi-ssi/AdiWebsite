Bangus Silog

Ingredients

For the Bangus (Milkfish):

  • 2 bangus (milkfish) fillets or 2 whole bangus, cleaned and deboned
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp calamansi or lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil (for frying)

For the Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice):

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

For the Itlog (Fried Egg):

  • 4 eggs
  • Oil for frying

For garnish:

  • Sliced tomatoes (optional)
  • Cucumber slices (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Bangus (Milkfish):
    1. Marinate the bangus: In a bowl, combine soy sauce, calamansi (or lemon juice), and ground black pepper. Add the bangus fillets and marinate for about 10-15 minutes.
    2. Cook the bangus: Heat the vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the bangus fillets and fry for 3-4 minutes per side or until golden and crispy. If using whole bangus, make sure to fry it until cooked through. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice):
    1. Sauté garlic: In the same pan, add 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil. Add the minced garlic and sauté over medium heat until golden brown and fragrant.
    2. Fry the rice: Add the day-old rice to the pan and stir-fry for 3-5 minutes. Break up any rice clumps and continue frying until the rice is heated through and slightly crispy. Season with salt to taste, then remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. Prepare the Itlog (Fried Egg):
    1. Fry the eggs: In a separate pan, heat oil over medium heat. Crack the eggs into the pan and fry them to your preferred doneness. Typically, sunny-side-up eggs are served with silog dishes. Season with a little salt and pepper. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  4. Assemble the Bangus Silog:
    1. Serve: On a plate, serve a portion of sinangag (garlic fried rice), top it with the cooked bangus (milkfish), and add a fried egg on the side. Garnish with sliced tomatoes or cucumber if desired.

Enjoy your Bangus Silog, a flavorful and nutritious Filipino breakfast that's perfect for a fulfilling start to your day!
