Lumpiang Sariwa

Ingredients

For the filling:

  • 1/2 lb (225g) ground pork or chicken (optional, you can also use shrimp or just vegetables)
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1/2 cup shrimp (optional), peeled and chopped (if using)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 cup green beans, thinly sliced
  • 1 small cabbage, shredded
  • 1/4 cup corn kernels (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2-3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1-2 cups water (for cooking)

For the wrapper:

  • 10-12 lumpia wrappers (can be bought at Asian markets or use fresh lettuce leaves for a lighter version)

For the sauce:

  • 1/4 cup peanut butter
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tbsp vinegar or calamansi juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 1/4 cup water (to thicken the sauce)

For garnishing:

  • Crushed peanuts (optional)
  • Fresh garlic, minced (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Filling:
    1. Cook the meat: In a pan, heat 1 tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and onion, and sauté until fragrant. Add the ground pork (or chicken) and shrimp (if using), cooking until browned and fully cooked.
    2. Add vegetables: Add the carrots, green beans, cabbage, and corn (if using). Stir fry for 3-5 minutes until the vegetables are tender but still crisp. Season with soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), sugar, salt, and pepper to taste.
    3. Simmer: Add 1/2 to 1 cup of water and let the mixture simmer for 5 minutes to cook down and soften the vegetables. The filling should not be too watery, so let the liquid evaporate if necessary. Remove from heat and set aside to cool.
  2. Make the Sauce:
    1. Prepare the peanut sauce: In a small pot, combine the peanut butter, soy sauce, sugar, vinegar (or calamansi juice), minced garlic, and water. Stir well over medium heat until smooth. If the sauce is too thick, you can add a little more water to reach your desired consistency. Add the cornstarch mixture to thicken the sauce and cook for another 1-2 minutes until it reaches a smooth, glossy consistency. Set aside.
  3. Assemble the Lumpia:
    1. Prepare the wrapper: If you’re using lumpia wrappers, separate them carefully. If using lettuce leaves, wash and dry them thoroughly.
    2. Assemble: Place a small amount of the filling in the center of each wrapper or leaf. Fold the sides of the wrapper over the filling, then roll tightly to seal. Repeat until all the filling and wrappers are used.
  4. Serve the lumpia: Arrange the lumpia on a serving plate and drizzle with the peanut sauce. Garnish with crushed peanuts and minced garlic, if desired.
